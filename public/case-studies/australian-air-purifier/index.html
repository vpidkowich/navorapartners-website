Client Case Study

Seeing Through the ROAS Mirage

This Australian air purifier brand came to Navora Partners in a state of confusion. Their Google Ads dashboard showed strong ROAS numbers from Performance Max campaigns, yet overall business revenue was declining. The leadership team couldn't reconcile the disconnect: if ads were performing well, why was the business shrinking? The answer was a classic PMax trap. The campaigns were reporting impressive returns by taking credit for existing customer purchases and branded search traffic that would have converted regardless of ad spend.

Behind the ROAS mirage, new customer acquisition had nearly ground to a halt. PMax was essentially cannibalizing the brand's existing customer base, spending money to recapture people who were already going to buy, while actual prospecting for new customers received almost no meaningful budget or optimization attention. The longer this continued, the more the customer base eroded through natural churn without being replenished, creating the revenue decline that had alarmed leadership. The brand needed someone to cut through the dashboard vanity metrics and expose what was really happening.

Challenges

The PMax ROAS Mirage

The brand was caught in one of the most common and damaging traps in modern paid media: Performance Max reporting impressive ROAS while actually cannibalizing existing customers and starving new acquisition of budget.

  • PMax ROAS mirage masking real performance: Performance Max was reporting strong returns by claiming credit for conversions from existing customers and branded searches, creating a false picture of campaign effectiveness.
  • New customer acquisition being starved: With PMax absorbing budget to recapture existing customers, there was minimal effective spend reaching genuine prospects who had never purchased before.
  • Revenue in active decline: Natural customer churn was outpacing new customer acquisition, causing a steady erosion of the revenue base that ROAS metrics completely failed to surface.
  • Overreliance on branded/retargeting traffic: The vast majority of ad-attributed revenue came from people who already knew the brand, meaning paid media was functioning as a tax on existing demand rather than a growth engine.

Solution

Exposing Truth & Rebuilding Growth

We started by proving what was really happening with a comprehensive ROAS reality check, then systematically rebuilt the channel strategy to prioritize new customer acquisition while maintaining efficient retargeting of the existing base.

1

ROAS Reality Check

Conducted a forensic analysis of PMax performance, cross-referencing platform-reported conversions with backend data to expose how much "attributed" revenue was actually incremental versus cannibalized.

2

Channel Separation

Broke the monolithic PMax structure into distinct channels with clear roles: branded defense, retargeting, and true prospecting, each with separate budgets and performance expectations.

3

New Customer Engine

Built a dedicated prospecting infrastructure with proper audience targeting, creative tailored to cold audiences, and nCAC-based optimization that ensured every dollar drove genuine new business.

4

P&L Reconciliation

Aligned all media reporting with actual business P&L, replacing platform vanity metrics with financial outcomes that leadership could trust and act on with confidence.

Results

Decline Reversed to Growth

By exposing the ROAS mirage and redirecting budget toward genuine new customer acquisition, the brand reversed its revenue decline and established a sustainable growth trajectory. The $98 nCAC reduction meant the business was acquiring customers at a fraction of the previous cost, making every scaling dollar dramatically more productive.
