CLIENT CASE STUDY

The 30,000 SKU Bottleneck

Our client is a premier online retailer specializing in automotive suspension and shocks, managing a massive catalog of over 30,000 SKUs across 21 brands. While the business was successful, they were sitting on a powder keg of potential during the global e-commerce boom. They were ready to dominate the market but lacked the campaign infrastructure to manage their inventory at scale.

The sheer volume of data had paralyzed their previous strategy. Their entire 30,000 SKU catalog was dumped into a single Google Shopping campaign — forcing the algorithm to make broad, inefficient bidding decisions, often ignoring high-margin suspension kits in favor of cheap, high-click traffic that rarely converted. The winners were suffocating under the weight of thousands of “zombie” products.

With the market demand for auto parts exploding during the e-commerce boom, every day without a segmented strategy meant market share handed to competitors with better-organized accounts.

Challenges

The One-Bucket Trap

  • The entire 30,000 SKU catalog was dumped into a single Google Shopping campaign, forcing broad, inefficient bidding decisions
  • High-margin suspension kits were buried under thousands of “zombie” products that rarely converted
  • No ability to prioritize “Top Movers” — the products that actually drove profit
  • Search Impression Share stuck at 40%, hemorrhaging visibility to competitors with better-organized accounts
  • The market demand for auto parts was exploding, but without segmentation they were leaving market share on the table

Solution

A Brand-Tiered Segmentation Matrix

1
Profit-First Prioritization

As a reseller, not all brands yield the same margin. We ignored the “long tail” initially and focused resources on the Most Profitable Brands, ensuring these high-margin lines had 100% coverage across both Search and Shopping.

2
Series-Level Granularity

Simply organizing by brand wasn’t enough. We drilled down into product series and price points so a customer searching for a premium performance kit wasn’t shown an entry-level shock absorber. This drastically increased ad relevance and CTR.

3
Waterfall Expansion

Once the high-profit brands were stabilized, we expanded to high-volume brands using a “waterfall” approach, slowly working down to lower-visibility products without destabilizing what was already working.

4
Custom Label Logic

We utilized Custom Labels in the product feed to tag items dynamically as “Top Seller,” “High Profit,” or “High Volume.” This allowed aggressive bidding on winners while suppressing bids on low-margin inventory — without manually adjusting thousands of product groups.

Results

Dominating Market Share with Lean Efficiency

In the low-margin world of auto parts reselling, volume means nothing without profitability. By cutting wasted spend on “zombie products” and funneling budget toward high-margin brands, we achieved a rare “double win”: skyrocketing revenue while driving down acquisition costs.

  • Revenue scaled from $280K to $1.5M per month (+435%)
  • nCAC decreased 30% within the first 3 months
  • Maintained 10x MER ensuring the bottom line grew as fast as the top line
  • Search Impression Share increased from 40% to 54%
  • Total order volume doubled within the first 6 months
